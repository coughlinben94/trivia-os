Sequence (~4.85s): ground rumble (700ms — dusk, ship idling on its landing legs with a persistent small jitter, not a decaying shake — reads as "about to take off," not an impact; letterbox bars settle in) → glide to window (1100ms — the whole world scales up around a fixed point at the cockpit window's exact screen position, ease-in-out, so it reads as one continuous camera push rather than a generic zoom; the captain silhouette fades in only in the last ~45% of this beat — a glimpse, not a held shot) → POV cut (350ms — the captain glimpse cuts away fast in the first quarter of this beat, the ship hull fades out, the cockpit rim/dashboard/pilot-seat silhouette fade in) → ascent (2200ms — ground drops away and fades, sky darkens from dusk through meteor-shower's own navy-night palette, stars fade in and drift from icy-white toward team-picker's fixed grayscale) → handoff hold (500ms — fully resolved to team-picker's exact #000000 bg / #e8e8e8-#3a3a3a grayscale stars, "Now let's meet our teams" caption fades in) — at this exact frame the real handoff crossfades into TeamPickerSlide itself.

Why a passenger POV needs a visible cue at all: a bare cockpit frame with no pilot reads ambiguous — is no one flying this thing? The pilot-seat silhouette in the cockpit's lower-left corner (present the whole interior POV, not just a flash) is the minimal, non-literal way to keep "you're a passenger, someone else is driving" legible without any on-screen text, per the metaphor-over-literal-explanation principle.

Reused, not invented in parallel: TeamPickerSlide.jsx is explicitly documented in its own source as theme-agnostic by design ("black background, grayscale-to-white stars... a consistent ceremony moment") — so rather than touching that component or inventing a competing "themed" warp look for the handoff, this file's whole job is to arrive at that exact palette by its last frame so the crossfade is invisible. That's "design as an extension" applied to a color endpoint, not a shared engine.

Known rough edges, not hidden: the cockpit rim/dashboard/pilot-seat/captain silhouette are hand-drawn CSS shapes, not a generated asset — a real interior render (same Recraft pipeline, digital_illustration style) would read more crafted than flat CSS gradients. The captain silhouette's screen position is computed algebraically from the same eyeballed window-offset constant as the round-boundary file's impact point — not independently verified against where the sprite's actual window sits.

A Fable contrast/position/timing pass caught eleven real issues; all eleven are fixed in this revision: (1) the caption's z-index:8 collided with the bottom letterbox bar sharing the same z-index and coming later in the DOM — the caption never actually rendered; moved to z-index:9/bottom:12%; (2) every cockpit shape was #050608 on a near-black vignette (~1.04:1 contrast — invisible), and the pilot seat sat entirely outside the rim's own left edge — recolored the seat, added a second warm/cool rim ring keyed live to nightT, and moved the seat inside the rim; (3) the dashboard had zero instrument lights despite the notes' own "no dashboard-light flicker" complaint — added four, blinking on irregular 1.7/2.3/3.1/2.6s cycles; (4) the background canvas was a sibling of #world and never moved at all during the 9.5x glide, so the "camera push" only ever moved the foreground — it now scales too, capped at 1.5x, anchored to the same window-point transform-origin; (5) the rumble's decay was a flat per-frame multiplier (frame-rate dependent) and its jitter itself was raw per-frame Math.random() — both replaced with time-based dual-sine noise decayed via Math.pow(rate, dtn); (6) this file never computed dtn at all despite the notes implying frame-timing discipline — added, matching every other file's convention; (7) the dusk-to-navy horizon lerp passed straight through a muddy purple-brown band with zero stars anywhere near it (stars were capped to the top 62% of the frame) — added an intermediate plum waypoint, extended the star field to full height, and moved the star-alpha ramp earlier; (8) the shared grain overlay used mix-blend-mode:overlay, which is close to inert against a near-black background — switched to screen; (9) the captain silhouette read as a generic person-icon — added a small earcup and mic-stub detail; (10) the dashboard glow bar was centered on the frame's 50% axis while the rim itself sits at 58% (a real ~77px misalignment) — recentered the glow on the rim's own axis; (11) nightT/dropT were linear despite the ascent needing to read as accelerating away from the ground — both now ease in.

Also added, following a reference Ben pointed at as loose inspiration: a cartoon rocket-launch reel supplied two cues used at restrained scale rather than copied outright — a faint pre-launch exhaust flicker under the ship during the ground-rumble/glide beats (anticipation, since this file cuts to POV before any actual launch burn plays), and two small, muted, slow-drifting background "planets" during ascent (a scaled-down nod to the reel's own busier field of passing worlds). Separately, the ascent's starfield was upgraded from a flat twinkle field to a real 3D-projection hyperdrive starfield — the vanilla canvas/rAF technique Ben flagged from a Kirupa reference — which now runs for the ascent + handoff-hold beats specifically and directly answers this file's own previously-flagged rough edge: there was no sense of forward motion/G-force during ascent beyond a color shift. It ramps up through ascent and decelerates to a near-stop by the end of handoff-hold, so the "arrival" reads as a real deceleration, not a hard cut.

Reduced motion: the whole rumble/glide/POV-cut/ascent sequence is disabled — holds on the exterior ground frame (dusk, ship idle, no jitter, no captain), then hard-cuts straight to the team-picker-matched end state before the caption shows.
Self-gate
  • Extension, not a parallel system: ends by matching TeamPickerSlide's own fixed, documented-as-theme-agnostic palette exactly, rather than inventing a competing "themed" warp look for the handoff.
  • Real theme colors where the theme still owns the frame: meteor-shower's own night palette (bg #020408, highlight #e0f0ff, shinyAccent #80c0ff) is what the sky/stars pass through on the way to team-picker's fixed grayscale — not a placeholder gradient.
  • Replay button and reduced-motion toggle both clicked and confirmed working, not just wired.
  • GPU-appropriate: canvas/rAF is used only for the sky-gradient/ground/star field/hyperdrive-starfield/meteor-streaks, all genuine per-frame numeric needs; ship/captain/cockpit/engine-glow/letterbox/caption motion is transform/opacity only, and both the foreground world push and the new background canvas parallax are one CSS scale() each, sharing a fixed transform-origin.
  • No pop-in: ship fades in already visible at rest (not scaling from 0), captain fades in rather than appearing instantly, ground fades and slides away, stars fade in, caption fades in, engine glow flickers up from zero rather than snapping on.
  • Passenger POV honored, not pilot POV: the pilot-seat silhouette stays visible in the cockpit framing's corner for the entire interior portion, not just a flash — the ongoing cue that someone else is flying this.
  • Anticipation honored: the rumble phase gives the eye something to read (idle jitter) before the camera starts moving, rather than cutting straight to motion.
