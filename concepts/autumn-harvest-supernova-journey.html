Ember supernova — autumn-harvest round journey

Extends autumn-harvest's own shipped anchor (the rising embers in AutumnHarvestAmbient, ParticleBackground.jsx) rather than borrowing team-picker's star-warp engine — a fire theme's journey should be built from fire, not space math. A ring of embers gathers inward (anticipation), holds a beat, then erupts outward as the round arrives. One theme, one motif, same "converge then release" idea as the meteor-shower-to-galaxy piece, reskinned entirely in ember/harvest tones.

Sequence (~5.2s): hold (500ms, embers float at their normal idle radius, calm firelight base — anticipation beat, nothing dramatic yet) → converge (950ms, the same 24 embers accelerate inward toward center on a staggered per-ember schedule, ease-in so it reads as gathering pull rather than a snap; each one arrives and holds a beat, not all at exactly the same instant) → supernova (1500ms, ~70ms after the last embers settle at center — a small parent-drives-child lag so the flash reads as caused by the gathering, not simultaneous with it — a white-gold flash and expanding ember-orange ring release, and the same 24 embers erupt back outward past their original radius as fading sparks; "Round 2" fades in and holds around the 35% mark) → disperse (950ms, sparks continue outward and fade on their own staggered schedule — some finish before others, no simultaneous cutoff) → settle (1300ms, only the calm background ember layer remains, "Round 2" still holding).

One continuous object per ember, not a respawn: each of the 24 hero embers is a single tracked object with its own idle radius, converge delay/duration, burst delay/duration, and spiral drift — it's rendered every phase, just evaluated at a different point in its own timeline, so nothing pops in when convergence starts or when the burst fires. The gentle idle float in the hold phase is the same object that later gathers and erupts.

Background stays background: a separate, much calmer layer of small drifting embers plus a soft warm base glow runs underneath throughout, at lower brightness and much slower motion than the hero embers — same depth-via-differential-speed principle as the galaxy piece's subdued star layer while meteors are the foreground action.

Deliberately not reused: autumn-harvest's falling-leaf motif (the other half of its shipped ambient anchor) isn't part of this prototype — this one is scoped to the supernova moment itself. A leaf-shower entry/exit companion (the equivalent of the meteor shower bracketing the galaxy arrival) would be a natural next piece, not built here yet.

Frame-timing normalization: included from the start this time (the dtn clamp copied from TeamPickerSlide.jsx per references/round-journeys.md), applied to the background layer's idle drift.

Reduced motion: embers hold at their idle positions throughout, no convergence, no flash, no ring, no burst — "Round 2" just crossfades in partway through where the supernova would have played.
Self-gate
  • Extension of an existing anchor, not a parallel system: the hero-ember math is a new per-frame use (converge/hold/burst) of the same idea autumn-harvest's shipped RisingParticle embers already establish — brightness, warm palette, upward-drifting sparks — not team-picker's star-warp borrowed and recolored.
  • Real theme colors only: bg #1a0800, bgDeep #0e0400, accent #7a2808, highlight #ff6820, text #f8d8b0, plus shinyAccent #e8a020 for the hot flash core — all from autumn-harvest's actual entry in themes/index.js.
  • Replay button and reduced-motion toggle both tested working.
  • Canvas/rAF justified by genuine per-frame numeric need (24 independently-timed converge/burst paths plus a radial flash/ring) — same precedent as team-picker and winner-reveal.
  • No pop-in: embers are continuously-tracked single objects across every phase; round label crossfades rather than snapping in.
  • Edge margin respected: round-label text stays centered with real margin; only embers and the base glow (atmosphere) are allowed to travel off-frame.
